\section{Purpose of the Requirements Document}
  A product requirements document (PRD) is a document containing all the requirements to a certain product. It is written to allow people to understand what a product should do. A PRD should, however, generally avoid anticipating or defining how the product will do it in order to later allow interface designers and engineers to use their expertise to provide the optimal solution to the requirements.
  \\PRDs are most frequently written for software products, which can be used for any type of product and also for services. Typically, a PRD is created from a user's point-of-view by a user/client or a company's marketing department (in the latter case it may also be called Marketing Requirements Document (MRD). The requirements are then analyzed by a (potential) maker/supplier from a more technical point of view, broken down and detailed in a Functional Specification (sometimes also called Technical Requirements Document).

% subsection Purchase Module
\subsection{Purchase Module}
\subsubsection{View Reorder Point Report}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*.]
    \item System can predict the reorder date and reorder amount (reorder point) for every book, and generate reorder point report according to reordering theory and the predicted data. The reordering theory use the formula that reorder point = stock consumption during purcahse + safety stock quantity. Reorder point theory predicts sales selling quantity according to probability, obtains consumption during purchase and determines the time and quantity of reordering, so that the stock is not lower than the safety stock quantity. So in theory there is no case where the stock is 0.

    \item Purchaser can view the reorder point report
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Purchase}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*.]
    \item Purchaser can purchase new books according to Reorder Point Report.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Handle Order Exceptions}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*.]
    \item  In theory there is no case where the stock is 0. In fast, the phenomenon that book that the customer wants to buy is not in stock and cannot be pre-ordered may exist. System will alert order exceptions to purchaser.

    \item Purchaser can add the book into reorder point report manually to handle the order exceptions.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{View Stock Warnings}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*.]
    \item When it is close to the order date of the reorder point, the system send the purchaser stock warning to remind him of reordering.

    \item Purchaser can view stock warnings to see what book needs replenishment.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{User Management Module}
\subsubsection{Handle Accounts Exceptions}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*.]
    \item When the account is abnormally logged in or the purchase is abnormal, the system will send the user manager corresponding accounts exceptions.

    \item User manager can handle account exceptions.
\end{enumerate}
